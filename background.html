Industry Background

Key Services of the Hospitality Sector

  • Lodging – Hotels, Motels, Bed and Breakfast; Market segments include leisure, business, groups, and long-stay; Marriot International, Hilton
  • Travel and Tourism – Transportation; Air, Boat, Cab, Car; TUI Group, Expedia
  • Food and Beverage (F&B) – Meals, snacks, beverages for immediate consumption; Ranges from quick eats to restaurants;
  • Recreation – Rest, Relaxation, and Enjoyment; Spa, Entertainment, Sports; MGM Resorts International

Key Sectors of Growth

  • Hospitality sectors are conglomerate of other industry subsectors
  • Lodging – Worldwide forecast is $1.22tr; $1.21tr in 2019 (statista.com)
  • Travel and Tourism – Overall global size $9.25tr (statista.com)
  • Food and Beverage (F&B) – Forecasted $2.13tr in 2021; Expected 5% CAGR growth till at least 2025 (statista.com)
  • Recreation – Multi-Trillion-dollar industry with expected growth after COVID

Key Growth Drivers

  • Covid reopening
  • New Markets and Travelers; i.e., Asian Pacific didn’t travel as much for leisure as they do now
  • Cost of travel and ease of use has drastically improved
  • Business travel is increasing due to globalization of industries
  • Modern knowledge works allows for travel and consumers are looking for experiential travels

Key Technologies and Exploits

  • Server/Client Systems – Traditional Information Systems; exploits include MS server vulnerabilities and exploits
  • Network Devices – Used for Telcom (VOIP), Streaming, On-demand and Broadcast usage; Hijacking and traffic sniffing could be used for Social Engineering
  • Wireless internet offering in room and/or on grounds; Man in Middle exploits
  • Email/SMS (Messaging) – Delivery of Malware or Social Engineering
  • Mobile Technologies – Mobile Apps used for Digital Entry or room charges. Can be used to get services on someone else’s account; stolen credentials
